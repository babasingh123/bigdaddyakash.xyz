# NeetCode 150

The **NeetCode 150** is a curated list of 150 LeetCode problems organized into 18 topics that, taken together, cover the patterns most often tested in technical interviews. It was assembled by Navdeep Singh (NeetCode) as a focused upgrade over the older "Blind 75," trading a bit more volume for substantially better topical balance — every major data structure and algorithmic pattern has a small, deliberate set of problems instead of one or two scattered representatives.

This section of the site is a study companion to that list. It is **not** a solutions repository. For each topic you get:

- the prerequisites you should already know from the *Data Structures & Algorithms for Beginners* and *Advanced Algorithms* courses,
- the **core patterns** that recur across the problems and the mental models that unlock them,
- the **problem table** (name + difficulty) in NeetCode's canonical order,
- **tips** — the small details that separate a clean solve from a 45-minute fumble,
- **linked concepts** — pointers to the DSA-design problems on this site where the same data structure shows up in a system-design flavour.

## How to use this list

1. Walk the topics top-to-bottom. The ordering is intentional — earlier topics (Arrays & Hashing, Two Pointers, Stack, Binary Search) build the primitives later topics depend on.
2. For each topic, read *What this topic teaches* and *Core patterns* **before** opening the first problem. The patterns are what you are practising; the individual problems are just instances of those patterns.
3. Treat Easy problems as warm-ups (15 min max). Mediums are the meat of the list. Hards usually combine two ideas from earlier topics — when you get stuck, identify which two.
4. Cross-reference *Linked concepts* on each topic page when you want to see how the same data structure appears in design problems (e.g. heaps in `MedianFinder`, tries in `SearchAutocomplete`, linked lists in `LRUCache`).
